Typography

Typography plays a crucial role in brand identity. Below are three carefully selected font combinations that communicate luxury, professionalism, and distinctive character.

Option 1: Playfair Display + Dancing Script

Luxury Real Estate in Manhattan

Experience the epitome of New York City living with our exclusive property portfolio. From penthouses with stunning skyline views to historic brownstones with character and charm, we connect discerning clients with their perfect Manhattan residence.

Headline Font: Playfair Display

A serif font with dramatic thick/thin transitions and sharp details, perfect for headlines and creating a sense of luxury and timelessness.

Body Font: Dancing Script

An elegant cursive script that adds a personal, sophisticated touch while maintaining readability for short passages and accents.

Option 2: Playfair Display + Satisfy

Exclusive Properties in Tribeca

Discover the perfect blend of historic architecture and modern luxury in one of Manhattan's most coveted neighborhoods. Our curated Tribeca listings offer spacious lofts, designer finishes, and the sophisticated urban lifestyle you desire.

Headline Font: Playfair Display

Maintains consistency with its classic serif character while providing strong visual hierarchy and sophistication.

Body Font: Satisfy

A flowing script with a relaxed elegance and excellent readability compared to many script fonts, adding personality while remaining professional.

Option 3: Open Sans (All Purpose)

Upper East Side Luxury Condominiums

Presenting an exceptional collection of Upper East Side residences that exemplify Manhattan refinement. These meticulously designed spaces offer premium finishes, classic proportions, and proximity to Central Park and Museum Mile for the discerning homebuyer.

All-Purpose Font: Open Sans

A versatile sans-serif family with excellent readability across various weights and sizes. Its clean, neutral character works well for both headlines and body text while projecting a modern, sophisticated image.
